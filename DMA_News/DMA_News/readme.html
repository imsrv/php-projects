Installation

  1. Place all the scripts in a separate folder on your webserver.
  2. Open "dma_news_admin.php" in a text editor and enter your MySQL database configuration information in the config section at the top. (You can use either a new database or an existing database, all the tables that are created are prefixed with "dma_news" and will not overwrite anything in an existing database.)
  3. Open "dma_news_display.php" in a text editor and enter exactly the same information.
  4. CHMOD the scripts to something reasonable, such as 644, and protect the directory with .htaccess for security purposes. DMANews may get its own login routines in the future, but for the time being please ensure your own security.
  5. Open a browser and run "dma_news_admin.php".
  6. When run for the first time, the script will attempt to configure the selected database, simply follow the instructions on screen. As long as you have followed these instructions carefully you should get a list of successful upload reports followed by a link back into the script.
  7. Hopefully use of the script itself is self explanatory.


Templates

Admin Template

You can use this to integrate the admin control panel (dma_news_admin.php) into the design of the rest of your site. Simply enter all the HTML code that you want to use and insert XXX_CONTENT_XXX where you want the admin control panel to appear. The script then places the control panel within the template when the page is called (where you specified with XXX_CONTENT_XXX).

News Article Header
News Article Template
News Article Footer

This is where you specify the design of the news articles as they are output to visitors to your website. "News Article Header" appears once above the whole block of articles, "News Article Template" governs the look of each article and "News Article Footer" appears once at the bottom of the whole block of articles.

For example, if you have three news articles, the templates are called as follows ...

News Article Header
News Article Template
News Article Template
News Article Template
News Article Footer

"News Article Header" and "News Article Footer" are there primarily for use with tables (as [table] and [/table] respectively), but no doubt they can be used for other things such as copyright info.

Here's a better example of the table idea ...

(News Article Header) [table]
(News Article Template) [tr] [td] XXX_NEWS_XXX [/td] [/tr]
(News Article Template) [tr] [td] XXX_NEWS_XXX [/td] [/tr]
(News Article Template) [tr] [td] XXX_NEWS_XXX [/td] [/tr]
(News Article Footer) [/table]


IMPORTANT!

DMA News uses the following markers within "News Article Template" to insert information from the database ...

XXX_NEWS_XXX is replaced with the actual news
XXX_TIME_XXX is replaced with the time the article was written
XXX_AUTHOR_XXX is replaced with the author of the article
XXX_ALTCOLOR_XXX is replaced with once of the two alternating colors specified in the "Configure colors" section of the admin script.


Example:

[tr bgcolor="#XXX_ALTCOLOR_XXX"]
      [td align="left"]XXX_TIME_XXX[/td]
[/tr]
[tr bgcolor="#XXX_ALTCOLOR_XXX"]
      [td align="left"]XXX_NEWS_XXX[/td]
[/tr]
[tr bgcolor="#XXX_ALTCOLOR_XXX"]
      [td align="right"]XXX_AUTHOR_XXX[/td]
[/tr]


Note that XXX_ALTCOLOR_XXX changes between articles, not WITHIN articles. This means that in the example above, the same color is used three times, in the next article however, the other alt color will be used three times.

You don't need to use all the markers, you may not be using tables, in which case you probably won't use XXX_ALTCOLOR_XXX in your templates. Likewise, if you don't want to add the authors name, don't put it in the template.


Displaying the news on your site in a PHP script

Call "dma_news_display.php" as an include or require.

... using relative paths

      include ("./DMA_News/dma_news_display.php");

... using absolute paths, a little more difficult for the inexperienced

      include ("/home/~your_user_id/www/DMA_News/dma_news_display.php");

... or you could even try a URL ...

      include ("http://www.yourdomain.com/DMA_News/dma_news_display.php");


Displaying the news on your site in a .SHTML SSI page

To call as an SSI (server side include) in an .shtml or similar file use a virtual command.

      [!--#include virtual="./DMA_News/dma_news_display.php"--]
